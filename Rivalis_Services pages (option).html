Common Questions

Should I form a Delaware C-Corp or LLC?
Planning to raise venture capital? Delaware C-Corp. Small business keeping profits? LLC (more tax flexibility). Going public eventually? Delaware C-Corp. We assess your specific situation and recommend accordingly.
Why Delaware for incorporation?
Predictable corporate law, business-friendly courts, investor expectations, flexible governance options. Most VCs require Delaware C-Corps. Exceptions exist for companies never raising institutional capital.
What is an 83(b) election and do I need one?
If you receive founder stock subject to vesting, 83(b) election lets you pay taxes on current (low) value instead of future (higher) value. Must file within 30 days of stock grant—miss this deadline and face huge tax bills.
Can you fix a poorly formed entity?
Usually, yes. We clean up missing documentation, convert entity types, fix equity problems, and establish proper governance. Some issues (like missed 83(b) elections) can't be fixed retroactively. Earlier is better.

When should I file for trademark protection?
